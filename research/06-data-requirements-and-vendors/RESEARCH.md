# Data Requirements And Vendor Research

Date checked: 2026-05-04.

Scope: Phase 0 research only. This document does not authorize live trading, production data ingestion, or use of paid credentials.

## TRIDENT Data Ladder

TRIDENT-LOB has three practical data tiers.

1. Phase 1 minimum data: minute bars, top-of-book quotes, trades, optional news, corporate actions, and exchange calendars. This supports spread, midprice return, top-book depth proxy, realized volatility proxy for `k`, decay proxy for `epsilon`, and baseline prediction experiments. Recommendation: build Phase 1 against bars plus top-of-book first because Alpaca, Massive, Databento, IBKR, Tiingo, and Finnhub expose some combination of these data types at low or moderate cost, while full L2/L3 is usually paid. Sources: https://alpaca.markets/data, https://massive.com/pricing?product=stocks, https://databento.com/equities, https://www.interactivebrokers.com/en/pricing/market-data-pricing.php?menu=A, https://www.tiingo.com/kb/article/where-to-find-your-tiingo-api-token/, https://finnhub.io/docs/api.

2. Serious model-validation data: L2 depth with at least 10 levels, quote and trade timestamps, corporate action and symbol mapping, market status, and news or scheduled events. Recommendation: use Databento or LOBSTER for a small paid historical study because TRIDENT source/sink and depth-field claims require order book depth that consolidated top-of-book APIs do not provide. Sources: https://databento.com/equities, https://databento.com/pricing, https://lobsterdata.com/info/WhatIsLOBSTER.php, https://lobsterdata.com/info/DataStructure.php.

3. Full TRIDENT verification data: L3 market-by-order or Nasdaq TotalView-ITCH-style messages with add, cancel, modify, execution, visible or hidden execution flags where available, order IDs, nanosecond or microsecond timestamps, halts, imbalances, and point-in-time symbols. Recommendation: use LOBSTER for academic NASDAQ-only reconstructed message plus book data, or Databento Nasdaq TotalView-ITCH MBO for a smaller self-service commercial research slice. Sources: https://lobsterdata.com/info/HowDoesItWork.php, https://lobsterdata.com/info/DataStructure.php, https://databento.com/equities, https://databento.com/pricing.

## Dataset Type Coverage

Bars: free or cheap bars are enough for Phase 1 scaffolding. Alpaca free, Massive Basic, Tiingo EOD, Finnhub, IBKR delayed or subscribed data, Databento usage-based historical, Binance/Coinbase/Kraken crypto can all provide bars or bar-derived inputs. Recommendation: use Alpaca or Massive for US equities bars, and use crypto only as an engineering fixture. Sources: https://alpaca.markets/data, https://massive.com/pricing?product=stocks, https://www.tiingo.com/kb/article/the-fastest-method-to-ingest-tiingo-end-of-day-stock-api-data/, https://finnhub.io/docs/api, https://developers.binance.com/docs/binance-spot-api-docs/rest-api/market-data-endpoints.

Trades: trade ticks are needed for realized volatility, order-flow imbalance proxies, and aggressor inference. Recommendation: prefer Databento if accurate trade aggressor side matters, because Databento states direct prop feeds provide trade aggressor side and SIPs do not. Sources: https://databento.com/equities, https://massive.com/docs/stocks/get_v3_trades__stockticker, https://docs.alpaca.markets/docs/market-data-faq.

Quotes: top-of-book quotes support spread, midprice, and depth proxy. Recommendation: Phase 1 should accept quotes from Alpaca SIP/IEX, Massive Advanced, Databento MBP-1/TBBO, or IBKR subscribed feeds, but should mark feed type and delay explicitly. Sources: https://docs.alpaca.markets/docs/historical-stock-data-1, https://polygon.io/docs/rest/stocks/trades-quotes/quotes/, https://databento.com/equities, https://interactivebrokers.github.io/tws-api/market_data_type.html.

L2 depth: L2 is required for reaction-diffusion depth fields beyond top-book. Recommendation: use Databento MBP-10 or LOBSTER level 10 to 50 for Phase 2 research. Sources: https://databento.com/equities, https://lobsterdata.com/info/DataSamples.php.

L3 message data: L3 is required to estimate limit order sources, cancellation sinks, execution sinks, and queue composition directly. Recommendation: use LOBSTER message plus orderbook files for NASDAQ academic research or Databento MBO for self-service paid market-by-order research. Sources: https://lobsterdata.com/info/DataStructure.php, https://lobsterdata.com/info/HowDoesItWork.php, https://databento.com/equities.

News: news is optional in Phase 1 but necessary for the model's exogenous forcing term `N_t`. Recommendation: start with Alpaca or Tiingo/Finnhub news for timestamped symbol news and keep the interface provider-neutral. Sources: https://docs.alpaca.markets/docs/historical-news-data, https://status.tiingo.com/, https://finnhub.io/docs/api.

Corporate actions: split and dividend adjustment is mandatory for any multi-day or multi-symbol backtest. Recommendation: use vendor-provided corporate action endpoints when available and store both raw and adjusted prices with adjustment metadata. Sources: https://alpaca.markets/data, https://massive.com/pricing?product=stocks, https://databento.com/equities, https://www.tiingo.com/kb/article/the-fastest-method-to-ingest-tiingo-end-of-day-stock-api-data/.

Trading calendars: calendars are required to avoid future data and incorrect bar windows. Recommendation: use official exchange calendars as the authority and `exchange_calendars` as a local CPU-only helper after validating holidays and early closes. Sources: https://www.nyse.com/markets/hours-calendars, https://pypi.org/project/exchange-calendars/.

## Free, Low-Cost, And Paid Build Boundaries

What can be built free: data adapter interfaces, event store schema, synthetic data fixtures, crypto order book replay fixtures, US equity minute-bar baseline, IEX-only Alpaca quote/trade baseline, free Massive EOD/minute aggregate tests, calendar helper tests, and credential loading conventions. Recommendation: free builds must label venue coverage and delay so results are not mistaken for consolidated US equity evidence. Sources: https://alpaca.markets/data, https://massive.com/pricing?product=stocks, https://developers.binance.com/docs/binance-spot-api-docs/web-socket-streams.

What requires paid data: SIP recent quotes/trades, consolidated real-time US equity quotes, paid historical trade/quote archives at scale, L2 depth, L3 messages, full Nasdaq TotalView-ITCH, and serious historical news archives. Recommendation: do not attempt to validate TRIDENT source/sink equations on free IEX-only or crypto-only data. Sources: https://docs.alpaca.markets/docs/market-data-faq, https://databento.com/equities, https://lobsterdata.com/info/AccessOptions.php, https://www.nasdaq.com/solutions/data/equities/nasdaq-totalview.

Cheapest serious source/sink dataset: if eligible, LOBSTER academic flat-rate access is the best full-depth NASDAQ source/sink dataset for a research group at 4,798 EUR/year excluding VAT. If not eligible, Databento usage-based historical Nasdaq TotalView-ITCH MBO is the most practical self-service route because the pricing page advertises $125 historical credits and examples where Nasdaq TotalView-ITCH MBO can be sampled with signup credits. Sources: https://lobsterdata.com/info/AccessOptions.php, https://databento.com/pricing.

Enough data for Phase 1: Alpaca Free plus Massive Basic is enough for interface development and simple bars-only baselines, but a more credible Phase 1 should use Alpaca Algo Trader Plus or Massive Advanced for recent consolidated quotes/trades, or Databento historical MBP-1/TBBO/trades for a fixed research window. Sources: https://alpaca.markets/data, https://massive.com/pricing?product=stocks, https://databento.com/equities.

Data required for full TRIDENT verification: timestamped add/cancel/modify/execution events with order IDs, queue-level or market-by-order reconstruction, L2/L3 book states, top-of-book and deep depth, aggressor side, halts, auction imbalances, corporate actions, symbol mapping, and news/scheduled event timestamps. Recommendation: treat anything below L3 as partial validation only. Sources: https://lobsterdata.com/info/DataStructure.php, https://lobsterdata.com/info/HowDoesItWork.php, https://databento.com/equities, https://www.nasdaq.com/solutions/data/equities/nasdaq-totalview.

## Mac M3 Credential Storage

Recommendation: store API keys and paid data credentials outside the repository, preferably in macOS Keychain or a password manager CLI, with local development loading via environment variables. Never commit `.env`, downloaded vendor agreements, entitlement tokens, or paid data. Sources: https://support.apple.com/guide/keychain-access/welcome/mac, https://developer.1password.com/docs/cli, https://www.tiingo.com/kb/article/where-to-find-your-tiingo-api-token/.

Recommendation: keep a local `.env.example` with variable names only, load actual secrets from Keychain, `op read`, or shell environment, and use `chmod 600` for any temporary local-only secret file. Sources: https://support.apple.com/guide/keychain-access/welcome/mac, https://developer.1password.com/docs/cli.

Recommendation: segregate paper-trading credentials from live brokerage credentials, and block live-trading adapters in Phase 0 and Phase 1 until project validation gates explicitly allow them. Sources: https://docs.alpaca.markets/docs/market-data-faq, https://www.interactivebrokers.com/campus/ibkr-api-page/market-data-subscriptions/.
